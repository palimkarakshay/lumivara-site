# Accountants — solo bookkeeping → small CPA firm sales template

> _Lane: 🛠 Pipeline — operator-only sales positioning. Persona alignment: P1 ("premium solo / small-boutique professional", `../../research/04-client-personas.md §P1`). Default tier: T1 for solo bookkeeper; T2 for CPA practice with multi-staff._

## §1 — Snapshot

**Vertical.** Accounting & bookkeeping practice — solo bookkeeper / tax-preparer, 2–4 person tax-and-bookkeeping shop, small CPA firm (4–15 staff), specialised practice (corporate-tax, estate / trust, US-cross-border, not-for-profit). Out of scope: Big 4, mid-market consultancies (procurement runs through a marketing department).

**Persona alignment.** P1 — "the premium solo / small-boutique professional." Owner is the chief technical resource and the chief rainmaker; marketing is one more line item competing for time during non-tax-season.

**Default tier.** **Tier 1** (CAD $2,400 setup + $99/mo) for a solo bookkeeper with limited content needs; **Tier 2** (CAD $4,500 + $249/mo) for a CPA practice with insights cadence + careers/hire page; **Tier 3** for multi-office or bilingual.

**Headline pain in one sentence.** "We last touched the website two tax seasons ago, our 'services' page still says we're not taking new clients (we are, since June), and we have no good way to publish a 'CRA changed the home-office deduction' note for our existing clients without it being a two-week project."

## §2 — Who they are

**Practice shape.**
- **Solo bookkeeper / tax-preparer** — 1 person, often working from home or shared workspace. 30–80 active clients.
- **Tax-shop** — 2–4 staff, seasonal scaling for Jan–April, 100–400 active clients.
- **Small CPA firm** — 4–15 staff (1–3 partners + associates + admin + co-op students). Mix of monthly bookkeeping retainers, annual compliance work, advisory engagements.
- **Specialty practice** — corporate-tax-only, estate / trust, US-cross-border, NPO. Smaller staff, higher per-client revenue.

**Geography.**
1. **Southern Ontario primary** — GTA, Hamilton, Kitchener-Waterloo, London. Dense CPA-Ontario licensee population.
2. **Canadian metro secondary** — Vancouver, Calgary, Edmonton, Halifax. Ottawa adds federal-government-contractor flavour.
3. **US tertiary** — NY, NJ, MA, FL, CA, TX. AICPA + state-board scope; same ADA pressure.

**Practice age.** 8–25 years. Built on QuickBooks Pro (later Online), Xero, or Sage 50. Site built 2017–2022; touched twice since.

**Owner archetype.** Detail-driven; tax-code-fluent; suspicious of "marketing." Reads CPA Magazine / Pivot / Journal of Accountancy / Tax Practitioner news. Will not respond to "scale your accounting firm" copy. Will respond to: *"your peer in the Hamilton CPA breakfast group just retired their per-edit invoicing on a flat retainer; here's the math."*

**Decision-maker(s).**
- Owner is budget holder.
- A second partner (in 2-3 partner firms) often has informal veto on brand decisions.
- Office admin / co-op student is the day-to-day update channel — same office-manager-gate pattern as other verticals.

**Seasonality (load-bearing).**
- **Jan–April** — tax season; do not pitch (owner has zero attention budget). Discovery calls scheduled in this window will not close.
- **May–June** — busiest sales window. Owner has bandwidth, just-completed-tax-season urgency on operational improvements.
- **Sep–Oct** — secondary sales window before year-end planning takes over.
- **Nov–Dec** — year-end planning conflict; possible but slower.

## §3 — Core issues / pain points

1. **"The site says we're not taking new clients."** A frequent state — small accountants closed their inbox during COVID, never updated. Lost-LTV per turned-away inquiry: $300–$1,500/yr in recurring fees.

2. **"We can't publish CRA / IRS rule changes quickly."** The home-office deduction change, the trust-reporting amendments, the new T2200 form, the IRS Schedule changes — clients ask, the firm needs to communicate, the site can't. The firm sends a mass email instead, and the email becomes the "site."

3. **"The fee schedule is missing or vague."** Some firms publish ranges (good — sets expectation); others publish nothing (bad — every inquiry is a 30-min call to disqualify or qualify). Phone-edit makes a "from $X" disclosure cheap to maintain.

4. **"Our team page is missing the new associate / co-op student."** Same staff-page-staleness problem. Trust signal weak, especially for bigger client engagements where "we have depth" matters.

5. **"Online intake / engagement-letter signature is a paper process."** Clients fax or scan + email. CRA / IRS authorisation forms (T1013 / T2200 / Form 2848 / Form 8821) are a hand-handled paper trail. Modern accounting clients expect digital.

6. **"Clients want a portal — we don't have one and our practice-mgmt vendor charges $30/user/mo."** Karbon / TaxDome / Canopy / Aero Workflow have client portals; they are an add-on we wire to, not replace.

7. **"Reviews are flat — we ask, but nobody actually leaves one."** Accounting clients are loyal but quiet. Without an automated post-engagement review request, the firm's local-pack rank decays.

8. **"AODA / ADA compliance — we hadn't thought about it."** Lower regulatory pressure than medical / legal in immediate practice, but multi-staff CPA firms cross AODA's 50-employee threshold; ADA Title III filings include accounting firms.

9. **"Confidentiality of client financials — what does your AI see?"** Distinct from medical / legal: accountants worry less about HIPAA-style penalty schedules and more about client trust violation. The "your AI sees our client books?" objection is among the top-3 in this vertical.

10. **"We need a careers / co-op page that recruits."** Talent acquisition in accounting is a 2024–2026 industry-wide pain point — CPA designations are taking longer; firms compete intensely for co-op students and CPA-track associates.

## §9 — Regulator-of-record

**Ontario / Canada.**
- **CPA Ontario** (and CPA Canada-aligned provincial counterparts: CPA Alberta, CPABC, Ordre des CPA du Québec, CPA Atlantic). *Code of Professional Conduct* — particularly Rule 401 (Maintenance of the good reputation of the profession), Rule 402 (Advertising and solicitation), and Rule 403 (Professional and Office Disclosures). The advertising rule is materially less restrictive than LSO §4.2 — comparative claims are tolerated where factually demonstrable; testimonials are permitted where consent is documented. **Verify current text against the CPA Ontario CPD-update channel before quoting.**
- **AODA + IASR (O. Reg. 191/11)** — multi-staff CPA firms commonly cross 50 employees; in scope.
- **PIPEDA + provincial private-sector privacy** — see [`../../research/07-pipeda-breach-notification.md`](../../research/07-pipeda-breach-notification.md). Tax data is "sensitive PII" — breach-notification thresholds engage faster than for general PII.
- **Quebec Bill 96** — French equal-prominence requirement for accountants serving Quebec clients.

**United States.**
- **AICPA Code of Professional Conduct** §1.400 (Acts Discreditable to the Profession; advertising clauses). State-board CPA codes adapt this with material variation; **California State Board** and **New York State Board** are the strictest on testimonials and outcome claims.
- **Treasury Circular 230** — IRS practitioner-conduct rules; restricts claims about tax-avoidance schemes and unrealistic-position promises. A site claiming "we'll make your audit risk disappear" lands in §10.51 (incompetence and disreputable conduct) territory.
- **ADA Title III** — `[V] §B-ADA-Lawsuits`. Healthcare + legal are top-3 sectors, but accounting firms above 15 staff have been named defendants.
- **State accessibility statutes** (Unruh, NY HRL).

The pitch sentence: *"CPA Ontario / your state board takes advertising-policy enforcement seriously, especially around 'guaranteed' / 'expert' language. Right now your compliance is your office admin's memory + your last vendor's defaults. After we ship, every published change runs the policy lint, and the audit trail is dated for your CPA file or your AICPA peer-review."*

## §11 — Why they should switch

**Lead with R2, R1, R4.** Footnote R5 + R3.

- **R2 — Stop paying $200 per typo.** Accountants understand variable-cost lock-in and per-engagement billing intuitively. The 24-month math closes them.
- **R1 — Stop the silent decay.** "Site says we're not taking clients" is the canonical version of decay. Phone-edit closes the loop.
- **R4 — Cap the legal-liability surface.** AODA / ADA + CPA-policy + privacy. Less load-bearing than for medical / legal but still meaningful for multi-staff firms.
- **R5 (footnote) — Own everything you paid for.** Domain-lock fear common with CPA-Site-Solutions-class vendors.
- **R3 (footnote, solo bookkeepers only) — Stop being your own webmaster.** Solo bookkeepers run their own everything; they hear R3 directly.

**Do NOT lead with R6 / R7** — accountants have neither DesignJoy nor Webflow on the radar.

## §18 — Operator notes

**Never say.**
- "We can guarantee CRA / IRS audit-resolution outcomes." Treasury Circular 230 + CPA Ontario Rule 402 territory.
- "We do tax-firm SEO." We do site hygiene; SEO services are an adjacent vendor's lane.
- "Your client books are safe with us — we encrypt everything." Confidentiality is by *not seeing the data at all,* not by encryption claims. Don't accidentally suggest we ingest client books.

**Watch for.**
- The firm with **a 15+ staff multi-office structure.** Approaching agency-shaped procurement; T3 minimum, but be prepared to disqualify if marketing director resists.
- The firm in **active partner exit / succession planning.** Site rebuild competes with succession bandwidth; pause unless succession is in place.
- The firm running **Sage 50 only** (no QBO / Xero migration done). Often a tell that operations are dated; the website conversation may need to wait for a broader practice modernisation.

**After month 6 — what to upsell.**
1. **Insights cadence increase + niche service-area pages (T2 retention move).**
2. **Online intake + DocuSign / SignNow / Adobe Sign (T2 add-on).** First-tax-season Q4 sets up the demand.
3. **Bilingual (T3).** If GTA or Quebec firm.
4. **Tax-estimator widget (T2 add-on).** Q4 / pre-tax-season demand.

**Where the deal dies.**
- Calls scheduled in Jan–April. **Pause until May.** Rare exception: practice has a partner-driven mandate to ship before tax season — proceed only if the partner is the buyer and is calendar-blocking the project.
- Firm wants ad-management. Decline; refer.
- Firm in succession. Pause.
- Firm at >15 staff with marketing director who has a vendor preference. Offer T3 once; if friction, refer.

**Upsell cadence after month 12.**
- Month 12: T3 conversation if multi-office.
- Month 18: refer-a-firm — CPA Ontario CPD circles + AICPA SIGs are dense networks.
- Month 24: index-adjusted retainer renew.

## §19 — Specific-target search heuristics

> _Operator-only._

### Where to look (Southern Ontario primary)

1. **CPA Ontario "Find a CPA" public directory** filtered by city / specialty.
2. **Google Business Profile local-3-pack** for "accountant in [city]" / "tax preparer in [city]" — positions 4–10 highest yield.
3. **CPA Canada member directory** — cross-walk for credentials.
4. **LinkedIn search "Owner OR Partner OR Principal at CPA OR Accounting in [city]"** — practice owners' LinkedIn often links to their site.
5. **Local CPA chapter event sponsor lists** (CPA Toronto, CPA Hamilton-Niagara, CPA Western Ontario) — sponsors often have under-marketed sites.

### Where to look (US-Northeast / SE / CA secondary)

1. **State CPA-board licensee lookup** — public registries.
2. **AICPA member directory.**
3. **CPA Trendlines / Accounting Today firm rankings** — under-marketed firms in the long tail of regional rankings.
4. **Avvo / Yelp / BBB** small-firm CPA listings.

### Evaluation signals

```
[ ] Built before 2022
[ ] Mobile Lighthouse < 70
[ ] axe DevTools ≥ 5 critical / serious a11y
[ ] No AccountingService / ProfessionalService JSON-LD
[ ] "Are we taking new clients?" is unclear or buried
[ ] No fee disclosure / range
[ ] Tax-season hours not disclosed
[ ] No new content in > 12 months
[ ] Vendor name in footer ("Powered by <vendor>")
[ ] No careers / co-op page (or page is a 4-year-old PDF)
[ ] Engagement-letter / intake explicitly paper-based (PDF download)
[ ] Vendor lock pattern (CPA Site Solutions / Build Your Firm /
    CPA Practice Builders URL pattern)
```

**10+/12 = priority outreach.**

### Vendor patterns historically dominant

| Vendor pattern | Typical pitch | Typical delivery | Why we differentiate |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPA-vertical site builder (CPA Site Solutions / Build Your Firm / CPA Practice Builders / Rootworks-class) | "Industry-specialised templates," "monthly fee covers everything" | Templated site indistinguishable from peers; per-edit fee on top; vendor holds domain | Phone-edit unlimited; client owns code + domain; CPA Code lint built in |
| Local agency that "did our friend's firm" | Custom design | One-shot build; no ongoing CPA-policy expertise | Retainer with structural compliance lint |
| Practice-mgmt-vendor bundle (Karbon / TaxDome / Canopy with a website included) | "All-in-one platform" | Cookie-cutter front-end on top of practice-mgmt; design-constrained | We integrate with practice-mgmt; we don't depend on it |
| WordPress + a "we'll keep it updated" friend | Cheap | Nothing happens for 3 years | Pipeline-as-product; updates are guaranteed |
| DIY (Squarespace / Wix) | $17/mo | Owner spends weekends fighting editor | Phone-edit removes the labour |

Their pitch is 'industry specialisation' — what they actually deliver is one of three templates. We don't sell industry templates; we sell unlimited phone-edits on a real codebase."*
