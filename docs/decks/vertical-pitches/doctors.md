## What an outdated medical site costs you, today

You already feel some of this:

- **Wrong hours on Google + the website disagree.** Patients call the front desk to confirm. Front desk burns 3 minutes a call. Some patients don't bother and book elsewhere.

None of this is the developer's fault. It's a workflow problem: every change requires a five-business-day round-trip and a $200–$400 invoice.

## Built for the regulator your patients trust

Every published change is checked against the rules that govern your practice — automatically, before it ships:

- **CPSO / state medical board advertising-policy lint.** Comparative claims, superlatives, unsupported outcome claims — flagged before publish.
- **AODA / ADA accessibility compliance.** Lighthouse + axe-core run on every preview build. If accessibility regresses, the build fails — and you don't see a broken site reach a patient.
- **PHIPA / HIPAA-aware contact form architecture.** The form rejects PHI capture by default. Clinical questions are routed back to your booking system. We don't become a covered entity; we don't ingest patient health information.
- **Audit log dated for your file.** Every accessibility scan, every content change, every privacy review — recorded and retrievable.

Compliance becomes a property of the system, not a property of your office manager's memory.
