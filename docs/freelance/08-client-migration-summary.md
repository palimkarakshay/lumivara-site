# 08 — Already have a website? Here's how we work with it.

Most small businesses we talk to already have a site — Squarespace, Wix, Webflow, WordPress, Shopify, or something a previous freelancer set up. We have two clean paths and a hybrid in between. You pick one before we quote.

## If you want to keep your site

You're happy with how it looks. You just want **new capabilities** — a chatbot, a booking widget, mobile edits, an AI page, a careers section.

- We leave your existing site alone.
- We add the new capability on a subdomain you choose — `hello.<yourdomain>.com`, `book.<yourdomain>.com`. It runs on Vercel; your existing platform keeps doing what it does today.
- Where your platform allows it, we can drop a small script onto your current site so the new feature appears there too — without touching your CMS.
- You keep paying your existing host. We add our retainer for the new piece. Cancel either independently.
- You own everything we build — the code, the domain entry, the hosting account.

## If you want a fresh start

The current site frustrates you. Every change is a fight. The bill is climbing. Or the platform won't let us add what you actually need.

- We export everything — pages, posts, products, images, alt text — from wherever it lives today.
- We rebuild it cleanly, custom-designed, fast on phones, ready for monthly improvements.
- We map every old URL with traffic to a new one. Every link a customer or Google sent stays working.
- We stage on a preview so you review on your own phone before anything changes publicly.
- DNS cutover is a single screen-share — you stay logged in to your registrar, we walk through it together. No shared passwords.
- We don't decommission your old host until your first invoice has cleared. The old site is the rollback if anything goes sideways.

## Want to keep one piece of the old site?

That's a hybrid. We name the boundary in the proposal — which subdomain serves what, who edits which CMS, where each link points. Hybrid only works if both sides are explicit from day one.
