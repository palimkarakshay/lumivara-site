# 02 — Pricing Tiers (the four-tier ladder)

Four tiers, from "I just want to get online" to "we're a small firm and want this fully managed." Each tier has a one-time **setup fee** and a monthly **subscription**. The subscription is what turns this from a freelance grind into a real business.

> **Currency note:** all prices are in CAD. USD column is at ~CAD $1.00 = USD $0.72 (typical rate as of early 2026 — adjust at checkout). Fiverr displays USD by default; Upwork lets you set USD. Keep your books in CAD.

---

## The ladder at a glance

| Tier | Best for | Setup (one-time) | Monthly | Promise |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 0 — Launch** | Solo practitioners who just want a site, no automation | **CAD $1,200 · USD $880** | **CAD $0** (no retainer) | Beautiful site, full ownership, you maintain it yourself |
| **Tier 1 — Autopilot Lite** | Solo consultants who want phone-edits but not unlimited | **CAD $2,400 · USD $1,750** | **CAD $99 · USD $72** | Phone edits up to 5/month, quarterly improvement run |
| **Tier 2 — Autopilot Pro** | Most clients land here. Boutique firms, indie founders, growing practices | **CAD $4,500 · USD $3,250** | **CAD $249 · USD $180** | Unlimited phone edits, monthly improvement run, priority response |
| **Tier 3 — Business** | Firms with multiple sites, integrations, complex content needs | **CAD $7,500 · USD $5,400** | **CAD $599 · USD $430** | Everything in Pro + multi-site / integrations / SLA |

A fifth tier — **Tier 4 — Agency / White-label** — exists for agencies that want to resell. Don't promote it; quote it custom per deal at 2× Tier 3 minimum, with a written "credit my work" clause.

## Tier 0 — Launch — *"Just get me online"*

**Setup: CAD $1,200 · USD $880 · No monthly fee**

- 3 pages: home, services, contact
- Custom design (not a template), your branding
- Mobile + desktop, fast loading, accessible
- Basic search-engine setup (sitemap, social-share images, descriptions)
- Hosted on Vercel (free tier covers small-business traffic comfortably)
- Full ownership: you keep the code, the domain, the hosting account
- Handover: 30-min walkthrough call + a written guide
- **2 revision rounds**
- **Delivery: 7 calendar days** from receipt of intake form

**What's NOT included:** The phone-edit system. Future changes are billed at $90 per change (1-day turnaround) or you can upgrade to Autopilot Lite at any time.

**Strategic role:** This is your tire-kicker filter and your portfolio-builder. It's deliberately priced cheap enough to clear quickly, but not so cheap it attracts problem clients. Aim to deliver 1–2 of these per month while you grow the retainer base.

---

## Tier 1 — Autopilot Lite — *"Edit from your phone, gently"*

**Setup: CAD $2,400 · USD $1,750 · Monthly: CAD $99 · USD $72**

Everything in Tier 0, plus:

- Up to **5 pages** (e.g., add about + insights/blog)
- **Phone-edit system** — submit changes from a phone shortcut, get a preview link, tap to publish
- **5 phone edits per month included** (rolls over once; max 10 banked)
- **Quarterly improvement run** — every 3 months, I do a 2-hour audit and ship improvements (loading speed, accessibility fixes, SEO polish)
- Monthly health-check email: traffic summary, anything broken, anything I'd recommend
- 4 revision rounds during build
- **Delivery: 14 calendar days**

**What clients get on the monthly:** A site that actually stays current. No more "I'll get to that next month."

**Stop conditions:** Client cancels with 30 days' notice. They keep the site, lose the phone-edit pipeline (it stops accepting requests after the cancellation date). Their code, domain, and hosting stay theirs forever.

**This tier's role:** Where solo coaches, therapists, accountants, and side-hustle consultants land. Conversion target: 30% of buyers upgrade to Tier 2 within 6 months once they feel the cap.

---

## Tier 2 — Autopilot Pro — *"Just keep it running"* — **the headline tier**

**Setup: CAD $4,500 · USD $3,250 · Monthly: CAD $249 · USD $180**

Everything in Tier 1, plus:

- Up to **7 pages** including a custom landing page or case-study section
- **Unlimited phone edits** (subject to a fair-use cap of ~30/month — that's 1/day, more than anyone needs)
- **Monthly improvement run** instead of quarterly: every month I ship 3–5 improvements I've spotted, plus address anything you've flagged
- A blog/articles pipeline (MDX) so the client or their writer can publish long-form content
- Custom contact form with email routing (replies go straight to their inbox)
- A 30-min onboarding call after launch
- A 30-min "where should this site go next?" call every quarter
- **Priority response**: messages answered within 4 business hours
- Unlimited revision rounds during build (within scope)
- **Delivery: 21 calendar days**

**What clients get on the monthly:** A retainer-quality service for less than the price of one ad-hoc developer invoice. The site visibly gets better month over month.

**Stop conditions:** 30 days' notice; same as above.

**This tier's role:** This is where most of the money is. ~70% of your retainer revenue should come from Tier 2 clients within 12 months.

---

## Tier 3 — Business — *"We have several sites and integrations"*

**Setup: CAD $7,500 · USD $5,400 · Monthly: CAD $599 · USD $430**

Everything in Tier 2, plus:

- Up to **2 sites** included (a marketing site + a careers/booking subsite, for example)
- Integrations: Stripe / Cal.com / Calendly / Resend (email) / Crisp (chat) / one custom CRM webhook
- Cookie + consent banner (PIPEDA / GDPR compliant)
- Multi-language support up to 2 languages
- Quarterly strategy call (60 min) — content roadmap, conversion audit
- 4-hour SLA on production-down issues
- Stronger access controls (per-user phone PATs, audit log)
- **Delivery: 35 calendar days**

**This tier's role:** Boutique firms with 5–25 staff. They have a marketing site AND a careers page AND a booking flow. They want one accountable person, not a project manager + dev + designer trio.

---

## Tier 4 — Agency / White-label — *"We want to resell this"*

**Custom quote, minimum CAD $15,000 setup + CAD $1,200 / month / site under management.**

- You stay the operator; the agency presents to their clients.
- Written credit clause: your tagline appears in the site footer ("Forged by Lumivara") OR you get listed as the named technical lead in agency case studies.
- Capped at 5 client sites under one agency contract — beyond that, custom enterprise pricing.

**Don't promote this.** Only quote it when an agency reaches out. Most agencies who ask are looking for cheap labour; the steep price filters for ones who actually understand the value.

## Why monthly subscriptions, not just hourly

Two reasons.

1. **Predictable revenue beats lumpy revenue.** 20 clients on Tier 2 = CAD $4,980/month MRR. That's enough to cover your fixed costs and time without needing to sell another build. Hourly work doesn't compound; subscriptions do.
2. **The system rewards retention.** The longer a client stays, the more value the monthly improvement run delivers (compounding SEO, accumulated polish). The longer they stay, the cheaper they are for you to serve (fewer "where do I tap?" support tickets). Pricing rewards both sides for sticking together.

---

## How to handle the price objection

When a prospect says *"that's expensive"*, the answer isn't to discount. The answer is to reframe.

> "Right — let me put it next to the alternative. A traditional agency build for a similar 5-page site is CAD $5,000–$15,000, and after launch every change is a CAD $150–$300 invoice. Within 12 months you'll spend more in change requests than the entire retainer would cost. The retainer also bakes in improvements you'd otherwise have to *remember* to ask for. So it's actually the cheaper option — it just looks expensive on day one because the value compounds."

If they still balk, downsell to Tier 1 or Tier 0. Don't discount Tier 2.

## Client decision tree (use on a discovery call)

```
Q1: "Do you currently have a website?"
   ├── No  → Q2
   └── Yes → "Are you happy with how it looks?"
                ├── Yes → Tier 3 retainer-only conversation (port + autopilot)
                └── No  → Q2

Q2: "How often do you wish you could update it?"
   ├── "Almost never"               → Tier 0 (Launch)
   ├── "A few times a quarter"      → Tier 1 (Autopilot Lite)
   ├── "Most weeks"                 → Tier 2 (Autopilot Pro)
   └── "We have multiple sites"     → Tier 3 (Business)

Q3 (always): "Who else is involved in the decision?"
   └── If "my partner" / "the board" / "procurement" — slow down,
       send them a written proposal, don't push for a same-day close.
```

## When to raise prices

- After 5 five-star reviews on Fiverr → +15% across all tiers
- After 10 five-star reviews → another +15%
- After your monthly recurring revenue exceeds CAD $3,000 → switch new bookings to USD pricing only (eliminate the FX drag and the cheaper market)
- Once you have a waiting list — even a small one — raise prices until the waiting list shrinks to zero

The Fiverr algorithm specifically rewards rising prices that don't hurt conversion. It reads them as a quality signal.
